Elicitation as a noun, elicitation, countable and uncountable, plural elicitations. 1. The act or process of drawing out information responses or reactions from someone.

The teacher used careful questioning for the elicitation of student opinions. The survey focused on the elicitation of honest feedback from participants. Police avoided leading questions during the elicitation of witness statements.

Good interviews rely on the elicitation of detailed personal experiences. 2. In linguistics, the process of obtaining language data from speakers through prompts or questions.

The researcher conducted elicitation sessions with native speakers. Picture cards were used for the elicitation of vocabulary. Controlled elicitation helps linguists analyze sentence structure.

The study relied on elicitation rather than spontaneous conversation. 3. In psychology or education, the process of provoking or bringing out a particular response, feeling or behavior.

The activity aimed at the elicitation of creative thinking. Certain images were chosen for the elicitation of emotional reactions. Role-playing is useful for the elicitation of authentic communication.

The coach used questions for the elicitation of self-reflection. Summary Elicitation refers to the act of drawing out information, language data or reactions through questions, prompts or specific techniques.
